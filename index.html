Processors

The Central Processing Unit colloquially known as a processor is the brain of the computer. Through the Von Neuman Architecture the input is relayed via the instruction register to the control unit, which retreives instructions and passes them to the ALU for execution.

Modern day processors are all microprocessors, which means that their entire circuitry is contained on a single integrated circuit. In order to make computers cheaper, manufacturer's often employ SoC (System On a Chip) configurations.

Previously, all the major processor companies competed over similar products, however today, on desktops and laptops, Intel is king, with AMD occupying a nice space by creating highly parallelized processors. In the mobile space, the competiton is fast and furious, with players such as ARM, Qualcomm and Intel. In the embedded space, hobbyists love using AVR's while Industry always picks ARM
